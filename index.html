Online International Workshop on Machine Learning Applications to Images, IoT and Wireless Sensor Networks

WSNs and IoT networks are often deployed in regions with difficult terrains where the parameters and network statistics change continuously and frequently over time, such as a forest area. ML and AI techniques as such, are often employed to avoid unnecessary redesigning of network topologies and maximize the networks performance in terms of lifetime, in-network transmissions and end to end accuracy. Considering the traditional definition of ML i.e. “The development of computer models for learning processes that provide solutions to the problem of knowledge acquisition and enhance the performance of developed systems” for WSNs and IoT networks, this workshop aims at discussing energy efficient solutions for optimizing the in-network parameters of WSNs and IoT networks using scalable algorithms and techniques derived from ML methods.


The workshop's outputs will be able to sustain themselves on the research avenue, with the major aim being to make the algorithm and theoretical models function in practical and device level scenarios. The resultant products are expected to trade off energy (and thus environmental costs) with technological development. The use of sensors for weather prediction, animal tracking, pollution monitoring, surveillance systems, smart solutions for habitat monitoring and many more have made everyone the beneficiary of the technology in one way or the other. ML based methods as such, if used for optimizing the performance of such a system as a WSN or IoT, would directly and indirectly affect the life of every other human being. ML based optimization in WSN will open new frontiers in the crossover of the two technologies. The workshop is designed with the idea that the crossover would directly affect the research community which is already exploring the combination of the two greatest techniques of this time, and the indirect effect will be on the beneficiaries such as the customers of the products developed using these technologies.


Targeted Audience


This workshop is intended primarily for students from third year (BE / B.Tech / BSc / BCA) and postgraduate (ME / M.Tech / MSc / PhD) students in computer science and related disciplines. Additionally, candidates having previous knowledge of topics are also welcome. The prerequisites for attending this workshop is a basic understanding of Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence and WSN.
